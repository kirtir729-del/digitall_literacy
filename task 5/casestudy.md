# Cybercrime Awareness Case Study  
## Topic: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

### Introduction
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India today. It involves tricking users into authorizing payments or sharing sensitive banking details, leading to financial loss.

### Case Study Scenario
Riya, a college student, posted an ad online to sell her old phone. A buyer contacted her and agreed to pay via UPI. Instead of sending money, he sent a "Collect Request" and asked her to approve it to receive the payment. Trusting him, Riya entered her UPI PIN and unknowingly transferred ₹10,000 to the scammer.

### How the Crime Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The scammer contacts the victim through online platforms or social media.  
2. They pretend to be a buyer, bank official, or trusted service provider.  
3. A fake payment request or phishing link is sent to the victim.  
4. The victim is convinced to enter their UPI PIN or sensitive details.  
5. Money is instantly transferred from the victim’s account to the scammer.  

### Targeted Victims
- Students and young adults  
- People new to digital payments  
- Individuals unaware of UPI security practices  

### Consequences
- Immediate financial loss  
- Emotional stress and panic  
- Risk of further fraud if personal data is exposed  

### Prevention Tips
- Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money  
- Always verify payment requests carefully  
- Do not click on unknown links  
- Enable transaction alerts and security features  
- Educate yourself and others about online scams  

### Conclusion
UPI fraud is preventable with awareness and caution. Understanding how scammers operate helps individuals stay alert and protect their financial information.
