## DO’s (Responsible Practices)

- Think before you post – ensure your content is respectful and appropriate  
- Protect your privacy – use strong passwords and proper privacy settings  
- Verify information before sharing to avoid spreading misinformation  
- Maintain a professional tone, especially on public platforms  
- Use social media to build a positive digital presence  
- Respect others’ opinions and communicate politely  

---

## DON’Ts (Things to Avoid)

- Do not share personal or sensitive information publicly  
- Avoid posting offensive, abusive, or inappropriate content  
- Do not engage in cyberbullying or online arguments  
- Avoid oversharing details about your personal life  
- Don’t post anything that can harm your academic or professional reputation  
- Do not trust or spread unverified information  

---

## Conclusion

Responsible use of social media is essential for maintaining a positive digital footprint. As a college student, your online presence reflects your personality, values, and professionalism. Making mindful choices while posting and interacting online can help you avoid risks and build a strong, respectful digital identity.
